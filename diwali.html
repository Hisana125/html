DIWALI

What is Diwali?

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is a major Hindu festival celebrated across India and in many other parts of the world. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance.

Why is Diwali Celebrated?

The reasons for celebrating Diwali can vary by region and tradition, but some of the most common are:

  • In North India: It marks the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after defeating the demon king Ravana and completing 14 years in exile.
  • In South India: It celebrates the victory of Lord Krishna over the demon Narakasura.
  • In Jainism: Diwali marks the day Lord Mahavira attained nirvana.
  • In Sikhism: It commemorates the release of Guru Hargobind Ji from imprisonment.
