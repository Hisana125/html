INDIAN FESTIVALS

DIWALI-

Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is a popular Hindu festival celebrated across India. It marks the victory of good over evil and light over darkness. People decorate their homes with lamps and rangoli, exchange sweets, and burst fireworks. The festival also honors Goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth. Diwali brings families and communities together in joy and celebration.

GANESH CHATURTHI-

Ganesh Chaturthi is a Hindu festival that celebrates the birth of Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed god of wisdom and prosperity. It usually lasts for 10 days, during which people bring clay idols of Ganesha into their homes or public places, offer prayers, sing, dance, and share sweets like modak. BAISAKHI-

Baisakhi is a harvest festival celebrated mainly in Punjab and by Sikhs. It marks the beginning of the new harvest season and the Sikh New Year. People celebrate with vibrant dances, music, fairs, and religious ceremonies at gurdwaras (Sikh temples).
