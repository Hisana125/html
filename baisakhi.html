BAISAKHI

What is Baisakhi?

Baisakhi (also spelled Vaisakhi) is a major harvest festival celebrated mainly in the northern Indian state of Punjab and by Sikh communities worldwide. It marks the beginning of the harvest season and holds great religious and cultural significance.

When is Baisakhi Celebrated?

  • Baisakhi falls every year on April 13th or 14th (depending on the solar calendar).
  • It marks the start of the Vikram Samvat new year in some parts of India.

Significance:

  1. Harvest Festival:
    2. It celebrates the harvesting of the Rabi crop and is a time of joy and thanksgiving for farmers.

  2. Sikh Religious Importance:
    3. Baisakhi commemorates the formation of the Khalsa Panth by Guru Gobind Singh Ji in 1699. This was a pivotal event in Sikh history, establishing the collective body of initiated Sikhs.

  3. New Year:
    4. It is also considered the Sikh New Year and is celebrated with great enthusiasm in Punjab.
